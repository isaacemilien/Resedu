Key information are important points that are relevant to the topic you will discuss in academic writing. Important points for gathering key information are reading through:

  • Learning objectives
  • Criteria
  • Topic questions
  • Task words

Research is an important step in most academic writing to collect relevant information. When researching one should develop queries based on previously outlined questions. The questions should make sure your points are truthful, follow through to substantial information, and they should draw clear links between different areas in the topic. Questions words can be used to help develop questions, these include 'Who? What? When? Where? Why? and How?'.

From key points and research previously gathered one can formulate a plan to guide you through your writing. General way of creating a plan for piece of writing is to:

  • Mark out core elements that you want to highlight before creating points
  • Conclude the arguments that you want to go over
  • Formulate structure for main points
  • Understand how you are going to connect points that are made

Developing Solutions

Techniques to aid in you in developing solutions to issues.

While studying many problems occur, one may have to tackle these without the help of others, problem solving skills give you a honed toolbelt to approach these problems. Common problem solving strategies include:

  • Outline related information
  • Experimenting with different solutions
  • Sorting out existing knowledge
  • Break problems down into smaller pieces

Critically approaching information is to draw conclusions, outline connections, and build your own ideas about topics you come across Approaches one should take to critically evaluating information are:

  • Ask questions as you read
  • Search of parts that are related
  • Check quality of information source
  • Make apparent gaps and bias in information
